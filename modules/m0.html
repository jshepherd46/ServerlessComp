Before you start:
environment + vocabulary

Two short lessons that pay for themselves ten times over. You'll set up the tooling you use for the rest of the course, and you'll learn the GCP and serverless vocabulary the later modules assume.

⏱ ~90 min total 🔧 Browser + (optional) terminal 📍 Pre-Module 1
What you'll get out of M0

Two prerequisites, in order

Do these in sequence. Lesson 1 gets you a working environment so the hands-on parts of every later lesson work. Lesson 2 gives you the vocabulary — every term it defines lands in the course glossary, which stays one click away from every page.

M0 · Lesson 1
Set up your work environment
Google account & UH org access, the BAA conversation for HIPAA, billing & budget alerts, Cloud Shell vs. a local gcloud install, multi-project switching with gcloud config configurations, VS Code + Cloud Code, Docker Desktop for the container modules.
~45 min Browser · terminal (optional) Hands-on setup
M0 · Lesson 2
The GCP landscape
The seven service families on GCP — compute, storage, networking, security, eventing, ML, and developer tooling — and the serverless vocabulary (cold start, scale-to-zero, ephemeral compute, container, image). Every term defined links to the glossary.
~45 min Browser Vocabulary + service tour
Why this exists

The shortest path to not being stuck

Module 1 jumps into resource hierarchy, IAM, and architecture decisions. Without the M0 vocabulary in place, half the terms in M1 land as jargon — and you spend the next ten modules quietly compounding confusion. The investment is 90 minutes. You'll get them back in the first hands-on exercise alone.

M0 is also the only module without research-project anchors — by design. Lessons 1 and 2 give you the neutral vocabulary so M1 onward can lean into HIPIMR, Makawalu, HALO, ShepBERT, and PacificFed without re-explaining the substrate every time.
